üîπ POST 4 -- RELATIONAL MODELING (From CSV to database)

After validating data quality, the next step was designing the data model.
The raw data came as a single CSV where:

* Each row = one set
* Workouts, exercises, and sessions were all mixed together

Instead of keeping everything flat, I designed a normalized relational model, separating concerns clearly:

* workouts: training sessions
* exercises: unique exercises
* workout_exercises: exercises inside a specific workout (order + supersets)
* sets: the most granular level (each performed set)

This structure allows:

* Correct aggregations
* Reliable joins
* Future analytics without rework
* Scalability when new data sources (API, diet, adherence) are added

The goal wasn't academic normalization -- it was building a schema that actually supports analytics and production use. Once the model was defined, it was implemented in PostgreSQL on AWS (RDS) and validated step by step.

Data engineering really starts when structure becomes intentional.
